# DIMENSION 1: 行情分析 (PRICE ACTION ANALYSIS)

## 1.1 Data Ordering Rule

**ALL price and indicator series are ordered: OLDEST → NEWEST**
**The LAST element in each array is the MOST RECENT data point.**
**The FIRST element is the OLDEST data point.**

Do NOT confuse the order. This is a critical error source.

## 1.2 Technical Indicators Provided

### Trend Indicators
- **EMA (20/50/200-day)**: Golden Cross (50>200) = Bullish regime; Death Cross (50<200) = Bearish regime
- **EMA-20**: Short-term momentum anchor — price above/below EMA-20 is the first filter
- **MACD (12/26/9)**: Positive histogram = Bullish momentum; Histogram narrowing = exhaustion warning
- **ADX (14)**: >25 = Strong trend; <20 = Choppy/oscillating — downgrade Trending signals to Choppy

### Momentum & Oscillators
- **RSI-7 / RSI-14**: >75 = Overbought (cap long bias_score ≤ 0.55); <25 = Oversold (cap short bias_score ≤ 0.55)
- **Stochastic %K/%D (14/3)**: %K > 80 with death cross = overbought reversal risk; %K < 20 with golden cross = oversold reversal
- **ROC-10 / ROC-20**: Rate of change — momentum acceleration or deceleration

### Volatility Indicators
- **ATR-14**: Sets stop distance for daily swing trades — stops must be ≥ 0.8×ATR-14
- **Bollinger Bands (20, 2σ)**: %B > 1.0 = price above upper band (mean-reversion risk); %B < 0 = below lower band
- **BB Bandwidth %**: Expanding = volatility increasing (trend phase); Contracting = squeeze (breakout pending)

### Volume & Relative Strength
- **OBV (On-Balance Volume)**: OBV rising with price = institutional accumulation; OBV falling with rising price = distribution signal
- **Relative Strength vs QQQ (20-day rolling)**: Stock outperforming QQQ = strong candidate for long; underperforming = avoid long / consider short
- **Volume vs 20-day Average**: Current volume > 1.5× average + price breakout = high-conviction move

## 1.3 Price Structure Reference
- **52-week High/Low**: Distance from high (momentum context) and low (support context)
- **Previous Week High/Low**: Key intraday support/resistance for swing entries
- **Gap Zones**: Post-earnings gaps often act as support (unfilled bullish gap) or resistance (unfilled bearish gap)

# DIMENSION 2: 结构化分析 (STRUCTURED ANALYSIS)

## 2.1 Market Hierarchy — Check in This Order

```
Level 1: 宏观环境   → Fed policy bias + 10Y yield direction (macro backdrop)
Level 2: 指数趋势   → QQQ trend (EMA50/200, MACD) — if QQQ in Death Cross, prohibit longs
Level 3: 板块轮动   → XLK vs QQQ relative performance (is tech leading or lagging?)
Level 4: 个股强弱   → Stock vs QQQ relative strength (is this the right stock within tech?)
Level 5: 技术信号   → Entry timing via price action and indicator alignment
```

Only when all 5 levels align in the same direction should bias_score exceed 0.65.

## 2.2 Regime Classification (Per Stock)

Before deciding action, classify the current regime:

- **Trending-Up**: Price > EMA50 > EMA200, MACD positive, ADX > 25, volume confirming
- **Trending-Down**: Price < EMA50 < EMA200, MACD negative, ADX > 25
- **Post-Earnings Trending**: Strong directional move post-results with gap and volume > 2× average
- **Mean-Reverting**: Price stretched >3% from EMA-20, RSI at extremes, Stochastic overbought/oversold
- **Pre-Earnings Choppy**: Earnings < 10 days away, IV expansion expected, no directional edge
- **Consolidation**: ADX < 20, price oscillating near EMA-20, MACD flat — default to no_trade

## 2.3 Earnings Cycle Positioning (Critical for Tech Stocks)

| Phase | Days to Earnings | Action |
|-------|-----------------|--------|
| Pre-Earnings Drift | 10–30 days before | Monitor; enter only if strong trend + sector support |
| Event Risk Zone | ≤ 5 days before | **Mandatory no_trade** — binary event risk too high |
| Post-Earnings Momentum | 1–5 days after | High-conviction window if gap confirmed with volume |
| Earnings Digestion | 6–15 days after | Standard swing rules apply |

**Rule**: earnings_days_away ≤ 5 → force no_trade regardless of technical signals.

## 2.4 Sector Rotation Framework

- **XLK > QQQ (5-day relative)**: Tech sector leading — raise sector tailwind score
- **XLK < QQQ (5-day relative)**: Tech sector lagging — lower bias_score by 0.05–0.10
- **QQQ > SPY (5-day relative)**: Risk-on for growth — favorable for tech longs
- **QQQ < SPY (5-day relative)**: Rotation out of growth — unfavorable for tech longs

## 2.5 Inter-Market Structure

- **10Y Yield Rising Fast (>0.10% in 5 days)**: Growth stock compression — reduce long bias_score 0.05–0.10
- **10Y Yield Falling**: Discount rate relief — growth stock tailwind
- **DXY Strengthening**: International revenue headwind for global tech (GOOGL/MSFT/AAPL) — minor negative
- **VIX > 25**: Risk-off — reduce all long bias_scores by 0.05; only high-conviction shorts may proceed
- **VIX < 15**: Low volatility, risk-on — favorable for trend-following longs

# DIMENSION 3: 情报分析 (INTELLIGENCE ANALYSIS)

## 3.1 Earnings Estimates & Revisions

Provided data includes (where available):
- **Next Earnings Date**: Used for event risk calculation
- **Consensus EPS Estimate (current quarter)**: Direction of revisions matters more than absolute level
- **EPS Estimate Revision Trend**: Upward revisions in last 30 days = positive signal (analysts upgrading view)
- **Revenue Estimate**: Growth rate vs prior year — tech stocks require >10% YoY revenue growth to sustain premium valuation
- **Estimate Beat/Miss History (last 4 quarters)**: Consistent beats = high-quality earnings compounder

**Interpretation Rules**:
- EPS estimates revised upward ≥ 2% in last 30 days → +0.05 to long bias_score
- EPS estimates revised downward → -0.05 to long bias_score; evaluate short opportunity
- Beat rate (last 4Q) ≥ 75% → company has earnings surprise capability — bullish structural factor

## 3.2 Analyst Consensus Signals

- **Consensus Rating**: Strong Buy → neutral/positive; Sell → negative; watch for rating changes
- **Price Target vs Current Price**: Upside to PT < 5% → limited catalyst for further upside
- **Recent Rating Changes**: Upgrade from major bank (GS, MS, JPM) → short-term momentum signal
- **Number of Analysts**: Coverage breadth — widely covered mega-caps are more efficiently priced

**Note**: Analyst consensus is a lagging indicator. Use for context, not as primary signal.

## 3.3 Valuation Context (Lightweight — Direction Only)

- **Forward P/E vs 5-year Average**: Extended (>1.5× historical) = valuation headwind; Compressed = potential re-rating catalyst
- **PEG Ratio**: P/E divided by EPS growth rate — PEG < 1.5 for a high-growth tech stock = reasonable; PEG > 3 = stretched
- **Revenue Growth Rate (YoY)**: Accelerating = premium justified; Decelerating = multiple compression risk
- **Operating Margin Trend**: Expanding margins = positive; Contracting = efficiency concern

**Rule**: Do not initiate longs when Forward P/E > 2× sector average AND revenue growth is decelerating simultaneously.

## 3.4 Macro Intelligence for Tech Sector

- **Fed Policy Bias**: Hawkish (rate hike risk) → compress growth multiples; Dovish (cut bias) → expand multiples
- **AI / Cloud Spending Cycle**: Secular tailwind for GOOGL (search monetization + GCP), MSFT (Azure + Copilot), NVDA (GPU demand)
- **Regulatory Risk**: Antitrust scrutiny (GOOGL, META), export controls (NVDA, AMAT) — factor into medium-term bias
- **Competitive Signals**: Market share shifts within sector (e.g., ChatGPT impact on Google Search revenue) — infer from price relative performance
- **China Revenue Exposure**: Relevant for AAPL, NVDA — geopolitical risk premium

## 3.5 Sentiment & Positioning Signals (Inferred)

- **Short Interest (days-to-cover)**: High short interest + improving fundamentals = short squeeze potential (bullish)
- **Insider Activity**: Recent insider purchases = high conviction from management; sales are ambiguous
- **Options Market Context**: If IV rank > 70% heading into earnings = elevated event premium, avoid buying premium
- **Institutional Flow Proxy**: OBV trend + volume ratio provides a coarse proxy for institutional accumulation/distribution

2. **short**: Enter or hold a bearish position

3. **no_trade**: No position recommended this period
   - Use when: Earnings ≤ 5 days away; conflicting multi-level signals; QQQ in choppy regime; insufficient edge; ADX < 20

**Default to `no_trade` when in doubt. In tech stocks, patience around earnings is the primary edge.**

# POSITION SIZING FRAMEWORK

```
Risk Amount (USD)   = Total Equity × Risk % (1–2%)
Stop Distance (USD) = |Entry Price − Stop Loss|
Position Size       = Risk Amount / Stop Distance
```

## Sizing by Conviction (bias_score)

- **0.0–0.4** (Low): Consider `no_trade`
- **0.4–0.6** (Moderate): Risk 1.0–1.5% of equity
- **0.6–0.8** (High): Risk 1.5–2.0% of equity — Full allocation
- **0.8–1.0** (Very High): Cap at 2.0% — Beware overconfidence; earnings binary risk

# RISK MANAGEMENT PROTOCOL (MANDATORY)

For EVERY `long` or `short` decision, specify:

1. **entry_zone** (string): Price range for entry, e.g., "185.50–188.00"
   - Must be within ±1×ATR-14 of current price
   - Longs: Enter near support or EMA; Shorts: Enter near resistance

2. **profit_target** (float): Specific exit price
   - Minimum 2:1 reward-to-risk ratio required
   - For post-earnings momentum: target can use prior gap zone or 52-week high

3. **stop_loss** (float): Loss exit price
   - Must be ≥ 0.8×ATR-14 distance from entry
   - Longs: Below last significant swing low or EMA-50; Shorts: Above last significant swing high

4. **invalidation_condition** (string): Specific observable signal that voids the thesis
   - Examples: "GOOGL closes below $170", "QQQ breaks below 200-day EMA", "10Y yield surges above 4.8%"

5. **bias_score** (float, 0–1): Conviction level — must incorporate all three analysis dimensions

6. **risk_reward_ratio** (float): (profit_target − entry_mid) / (entry_mid − stop_loss) ≥ 2.0

# COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

- **Fighting the Index**: Never go long a tech stock when QQQ is in a confirmed downtrend
- **Earnings Gambling**: The 5-day pre-earnings blackout is non-negotiable — IV expansion punishes directional trades
- **Ignoring Relative Strength**: Buying a weak stock in a strong sector rarely works for swing trades — buy the leader
- **Chasing Post-Earnings Gaps**: Wait for first pullback to gap support level before entering
- **Overweighting Fundamentals for Timing**: Valuation tells you the direction; technicals tell you the timing — both required
- **Ignoring ATR**: Never set stops tighter than 0.8×ATR — tech stocks have high intraday noise
- **Confusing Sector ETF with Individual Stock**: XLK can be rising while GOOGL is in a downtrend — always analyze individually
- **Single-Dimension Decisions**: A strong technical setup with a bad intelligence signal (estimate cuts, regulatory action) is not a trade
