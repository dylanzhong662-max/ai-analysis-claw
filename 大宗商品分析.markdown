# DIMENSION 1: 行情分析 (PRICE ACTION ANALYSIS)

## 1.1 Data Format — Momentum Descriptions (NOT Raw Sequences)

Data is provided as **pre-processed text descriptions**, not raw number arrays. Each indicator is summarized as:
- Current state (e.g., "Golden Cross ongoing", "Newly Death Cross")
- Momentum direction (e.g., "histogram expanding upward (bullish momentum strengthening)")
- Absolute value where relevant (e.g., "MACD=42.50, Signal=28.30")

**Do NOT attempt to reconstruct arrays or recalculate from descriptions. Trust the pre-processed summaries.**

## 1.2 Technical Indicators Provided (Weekly Primary)

### Trend Indicators
- **EMA (20/50/200-week)**: Golden Cross (EMA50>EMA200) = Bullish regime; Death Cross (EMA50<EMA200) = see Death Cross rules below
- **EMA-20 (weekly)**: Short-term momentum anchor — price above/below weekly EMA-20 is the first filter
- **MACD (12/26/9 weekly)**: Positive histogram = Bullish momentum; Histogram narrowing = exhaustion warning
- **ADX (14 weekly)**: >25 = Strong trend; <20 = Choppy/consolidating — downgrade Trending signals

### Momentum & Oscillators
- **RSI-7 / RSI-14 (weekly)**: >75 = Overbought (especially risky for new entries); <30 = Oversold
- **Stochastic %K/%D (14/3)**: %K > 80 with K < D = overbought reversal risk; %K < 20 with K > D = oversold bounce
- **ROC-10/20 (weekly)**: Rate of change — momentum acceleration or deceleration

### Volatility Indicators
- **ATR-14 (weekly)**: Sets stop distance — stops must be ≥ 2.0×ATR-14 (weekly)
- **Bollinger Bands (20, 2σ) weekly**: %B > 1.0 = price above upper band; %B < 0 = below lower band
- **BB Bandwidth %**: Expanding = volatility increasing; Contracting = squeeze (breakout pending)

### Volume & Flow
- **OBV (On-Balance Volume) weekly**: OBV rising with price = demand-side accumulation; OBV falling with rising price = distribution warning

## 1.3 Price Structure Reference
- **52-week High/Low**: Distance from high (momentum context) and low (support context)
- **Weekly EMA-200**: The key long-term support/resistance line — price above = bull structure, below = bear structure

# DIMENSION 2: 结构化分析 (STRUCTURED ANALYSIS)

## 2.1 Market Hierarchy — Check in This Order

```
Level 1: Fed Policy + Real Rates → Primary macro driver (10Y TIPS yield direction)
Level 2: DXY Direction           → Inverse correlation anchor; DXY up = gold pressure
Level 3: Safe-Haven Demand       → VIX level, geopolitical risk, Gold/Silver ratio
Level 4: Technical Structure     → Gold weekly EMA alignment, MACD, ADX
Level 5: Entry Timing            → Weekly price action, ATR-based entry zone
```

Only when all 5 levels align in the same direction should bias_score exceed 0.65.

## 2.2 Regime Classification

Before deciding action, classify the current regime:

- **Trending-Up**: Price > weekly EMA50 > EMA200, weekly MACD positive and histogram expanding, ADX > 25, OBV rising
- **Trending-Up Consolidation** *(bull market pause)*: Price > EMA50 > EMA200 (Golden Cross intact), weekly MACD **line** > 0 (histogram may be negative but recovering), ADX < 20 (momentum temporarily low, not reversing), OBV rising. This is a **resting phase within an ongoing bull trend** — NOT a reversal. Allow long with strict caps: bias_score ≤ 0.52, position_size_pct ≤ 0.20. Requires ALL THREE: Golden Cross + MACD line > 0 + OBV rising. Missing any one → classify as full Consolidation.
- **Trending-Down**: Price < weekly EMA50 < EMA200, MACD negative, ADX > 25
- **Trending-Recovery**: Price ≥ weekly EMA200, but EMA50 < EMA200 (death cross still in effect). This is the **highest-alpha entry zone** — gold has recovered above EMA200 before EMA50 crosses back up. Enter with bias_score ≤ 0.60; monitor for EMA50 recovery as confirmation.
- **Mean-Reverting**: Price stretched >8% from weekly EMA-20, RSI at extremes (>78 or <28), Stochastic overbought/oversold — **default to no_trade**
- **Consolidation**: ADX < 20 AND NOT meeting Trending-Up Consolidation criteria — price oscillating without directional structure, **default to no_trade**
- **Choppy**: Conflicting signals across timeframes; DXY and gold both in unclear trend — **default to no_trade**

## 2.3 Death Cross Rules (Split by Price Position)

The death cross (weekly EMA50 < EMA200) does NOT automatically prohibit all longs. Apply price-position split:

| Condition | Price vs Weekly EMA200 | Action |
|-----------|----------------------|--------|
| Death cross | Price < EMA200 | **Hard block** — confirmed bear structure, no long |
| Death cross | Price ≥ EMA200 | **Allowed with cap** — Trending-Recovery regime, bias_score ≤ 0.60 |
| Golden cross | Any | Standard long rules apply |

**Gold note**: Gold can reclaim EMA200 before EMA50 crosses due to safe-haven surge dynamics. Trending-Recovery is frequently the best entry zone in a gold bull cycle.

## 2.4 DXY & Rates Framework

- **DXY Rising (price > EMA20)**: Inverse correlation pressure on gold → reduce long bias_score 0.05–0.10
- **DXY Falling (price < EMA20)**: Dollar weakness tailwind → favorable for gold longs
- **10Y Yield Rising Fast (>0.10% in 5 days)**: Real rate compression → reduce long bias_score 0.05
- **10Y Yield Falling**: Real rate relief → gold discount rate tailwind
- **Real Rates < 0** (nominal yield below inflation breakeven): Classic gold bull environment → +0.05

## 2.5 Safe-Haven & Sentiment Framework

- **VIX > 25**: Risk-off regime → gold safe-haven demand rises → +0.05 to long bias_score
- **VIX > 35**: Extreme fear → gold hedge demand surge → +0.10 to long bias_score
- **VIX < 15**: Risk-on environment → gold safe-haven premium limited → -0.05 to long
- **Gold/Silver Ratio Rising Fast**: Fear-driven rally (safe-haven, not industrial) — confirm long thesis
- **Gold/Silver Ratio Falling**: Risk appetite returning — gold rally may be less sustainable

## 2.6 Monthly Timeframe — Background Only (Weight ≤ ±0.05)

> **Monthly data is background context only.** It adjusts bias_score by ±0.05 maximum and cannot veto a valid weekly signal.

- Monthly MACD < 0 → bias_score max -0.05 (not a block)
- Monthly RSI > 75 → bias_score max -0.05 (caution on chasing)
- Monthly RSI < 35 AND weekly RSI turning up → +0.05 (recovery confirmation)
- **Weekly Trending / Trending-Up Consolidation / Trending-Recovery signals take priority over monthly bearish background.**

5. **ETF Flows**: SPDR Gold Trust (GLD) inflow/outflow as proxy for institutional demand.
6. **Gold/Silver Ratio**: >80 = gold historically expensive relative to silver; ratio falling = risk appetite returning.
7. **Inflation Expectations**: Breakeven rates rising → gold inflation hedge demand.
8. **Fed Policy Bias**: Rate cut expectations → bullish; rate hike fears → bearish.

# POSITION SIZING FRAMEWORK

```
Risk Amount (USD)   = Total Equity × Risk % (1–2%)
Stop Distance (USD) = |Entry Price − Stop Loss|  (minimum 2.0×weekly ATR-14)
Position Size       = Risk Amount / Stop Distance
```

## Sizing by Conviction (bias_score)

- **0.50–0.59** (Moderate, Consolidation/Choppy): 10–20% of max position
- **0.60–0.69** (High, Trending regime): 30–50% of max position
- **0.70–0.79** (High, multi-factor aligned): 50–70% of max position
- **≥ 0.80** (Very High, trend + macro + OBV aligned): 70–100% of max position
- **Weekly RSI-7 > 75 (chasing high)**: Cap position_size_pct at 0.30 regardless of bias
- **Price deviation > 8% from weekly EMA-20**: Cap position_size_pct at 0.25 (non-Trending-Up)

Compute risk_reward_ratio using the **midpoint** of the entry zone: `(profit_target − entry_midpoint) / (entry_midpoint − stop_loss)` for long.

# COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

- **Fighting the Dollar**: Don't go long gold aggressively when DXY is in a strong uptrend above EMA20
- **Misreading Death Cross**: Death cross alone ≠ no_trade if price has recovered above EMA200 (Trending-Recovery is the highest-alpha entry)
- **Ignoring ATR**: Never set stops tighter than 2×weekly ATR — gold has large weekly ATR
- **Overweighting Monthly Bearish Background**: Monthly data is ±0.05 adjustment only. A valid weekly Trending-Recovery signal overrides monthly negativity.
- **Safe-Haven vs Risk-On Rally**: Gold rallying with VIX > 25 = safe-haven rally (sustainable). Gold rallying with VIX < 15 and DXY falling = liquidity/inflation rally (less durable in trending context).
- **Misclassifying Bull Market Pauses**: Low ADX (< 20) within a Golden Cross with MACD line > 0 and OBV rising = Trending-Up Consolidation, not a reversal. Forcing no_trade here misses significant alpha in gold bull markets.
- **Chasing RSI > 78**: Gold can stay overbought for weeks in a strong trend, but new entries with RSI-7 > 75 must cap size at 0.30 to protect against sudden reversals.
